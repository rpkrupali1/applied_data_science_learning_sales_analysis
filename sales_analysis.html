1c: Data Normalization¶

Why Normalization over Standardization?

  • Normalization (Min-Max scaling) scales data to a 0–1 range. Best when data does not follow a normal distribution.
  • Standardization (Z-score) centers data around mean=0, std=1. Best for normally distributed data.

Since sales data is often skewed (a few states sell much more), normalization is preferred here — it preserves the relative differences and is easier to interpret (0 = lowest, 1 = highest).

1d: Aggregating Data¶

GroupBy Insights

GroupBy splits data into groups based on a column, applies a function (sum, mean, etc.), and combines the results. This is essential for:

  • Data chunking: Breaking sales data by State or Group to analyze each segment independently.
  • Data merging/aggregation: Summarizing total sales per state, average units per group, etc.

Recommendation: Use GroupBy for aggregation in this analysis — it lets us compare total and average sales across states and demographic groups, which directly answers the CEO's questions about revenue by state.

State-wise Analysis¶

  • VIC is the highest revenue-generating state, significantly ahead of all others.
  • NSW ranks second, followed by SA.
  • WA, NT, and TAS generate the lowest revenues and need targeted sales programs.

Group-wise Analysis¶

  • All four demographic groups (Men, Women, Kids, Seniors) contribute almost equally to overall revenue.
  • This indicates a well-balanced product portfolio across age groups.

Time-of-Day Analysis¶

  • Morning hours show the highest sales, indicating peak shopping time.
  • Evening and Afternoon follow — S&M teams should focus promotions and personalized offers during off-peak hours to boost sales.

Recommendations¶

  1. Focus expansion on VIC and NSW — these are proven high-revenue states and can support more branches.
  2. Develop targeted sales programs for WA, NT, and TAS — consider region-specific promotions, discounts, and marketing campaigns to boost revenue in these low-performing states.
  3. Implement hyper-personalization during off-peak hours — use Next Best Offer strategies during afternoon/evening to increase conversion rates.
  4. Maintain balanced group strategy — since all demographic groups contribute equally, continue the current product mix across Kids, Men, Women, and Seniors.
  5. Monitor weekly trends — weekly reports show sales fluctuations that can be used to plan inventory and staffing more efficiently.
